The Principal

Ian Cook, PhD

Nine years shipping AI systems across defense, healthcare, insurance, and consumer tech. Currently SVP of AI Technology at Qloo.

I've spent my career building things that work—not decks about things that might work someday. The gap between "promising prototype" and "production system" is where most AI projects die. I live in that gap.

Fierce Highways is how I help teams who need to move now—not next quarter, not after another planning cycle. You have a problem. Let's solve it.

Background

My path to AI was unconventional. I started in public policy at the University of Chicago, studying how people make decisions. That led me to RAND Corporation, where I did quantitative policy analysis for defense agencies. While pursuing my PhD in Political Science at the University of Pittsburgh, I taught myself Python and discovered that econometrics translated surprisingly well to the then-exploding field of data science.

Since then, I've led ML and data engineering teams across defense, healthcare, insurance, and consumer intelligence. At Sixfold, I improved document extraction accuracy by 40%. At SEEK and Govini, I built analytics platforms that turned messy enterprise data into actionable insights. Now at Qloo, I lead teams building cultural intelligence platforms that map 3.7 billion lifestyle entities.

I'm also the self-appointed Curmudgeon in Residence at the Auditory Perception and Cognition Lab—a title that reflects a curiosity spanning AI, data, and how humans actually think.
