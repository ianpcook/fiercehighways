## 1. AI-Powered Personalization — What Happens Behind the Scenes

*The show's tagline is "behind-the-scenes" — lean into the operational reality of building AI products.*

**Key talking points:**

- **What Qloo actually does.** Consumer preference prediction across entertainment, dining, travel, fashion. Explain it simply: "We teach machines to understand taste." Fortune 500 clients rely on this to make smarter decisions about what to offer, where, and to whom.

- **The data infrastructure nobody talks about.** The flashy output is a recommendation. The real work is ingesting, cleaning, and connecting preference signals across domains. Most AI companies are 80% data plumbing, 20% model work.

- **Why personalization matters for small businesses too.** You don't need Qloo's scale to benefit. Understanding customer preferences and using AI to segment, recommend, and personalize is accessible now — but it requires discipline about data quality from day one.

**Story to tell:** A Qloo client use case where the AI surfaced a non-obvious connection between consumer preferences — e.g., dining taste predicting entertainment preferences — that created business value nobody expected.

## 2. Cutting Through the AI Hype — What Business Owners Actually Need to Know

*Armando's audience includes non-technical business owners. Give them a practical filter.*

**Key talking points:**

- **The AGI distraction.** Every week there's a new "breakthrough." Most of it is irrelevant to running a business. The useful AI is already here — document processing, customer routing, inventory optimization. Boring but transformative.

- **The build vs. buy decision in 2026.** Off-the-shelf tools (GPT, Claude, Gemini) handle 80% of what small businesses need. Don't build custom unless it's your core differentiator. The money is in integration, not innovation.

- **Three questions every business owner should ask before any AI investment:** (1) What's the blast radius if this is wrong? (2) Can we measure success in business terms, not accuracy percentages? (3) Who maintains this after the consultant leaves?

**Story to tell:** The Highmark Health transformation — 97% reduction in underwriting time. Not because of a fancy model, but because they got the process, compliance, and human adoption right.

## 3. Navigating Career Pivots and Building in AI

*The show features diverse career journeys — Ian's path from RAND to healthcare to startups to Qloo is a great arc.*

**Key talking points:**

- **Defense to healthcare to consumer tech.** Each sector taught different constraints. DoD: accuracy is life-or-death. Healthcare: compliance is non-negotiable. Consumer: speed and personalization win. The common thread is building trust in AI outputs.

- **The startup reality.** Sixfold pivot experience — what it's like when the market shifts and you have to rebuild the product thesis. Most AI startups don't make headlines because they're grinding through product-market fit, not raising flashy rounds.

- **PhD to practitioner.** Academic rigor is a foundation, but the real learning happens in production systems with messy data, impatient stakeholders, and tight deadlines.

**Story to tell:** The Sixfold pivot — what triggered it, how the team navigated uncertainty, and what it taught about building AI products that survive market shifts.
