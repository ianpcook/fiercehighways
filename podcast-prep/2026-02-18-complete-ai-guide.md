## 1. Why AGI Is Noise — And What SMBs Should Focus On Instead

*This is what Ian pitched. Lean into it hard — this audience wants permission to ignore the hype.*

**Key talking points:**

- **The AGI timeline debate is irrelevant to your business.** Whether AGI arrives in 5 years or 50, the AI that matters today is narrow, task-specific, and already deployed. Document automation, customer segmentation, scheduling optimization — this is the real AI revolution.

- **The hype cycle hurts small businesses most.** Enterprise can afford to experiment and fail. SMBs can't. Every dollar chasing a flashy AI tool that doesn't deliver is a dollar not spent on growth. The filter: does this solve a problem I have today?

- **Qloo as an example of narrow AI done right.** We predict consumer preferences across entertainment, dining, travel, fashion. Not AGI — just really good at one thing, at scale, for Fortune 500 clients. The lesson: specificity beats generality.

**Story to tell:** A concrete example of how a non-tech business could use AI tools available today (not custom builds) to reduce costs or increase revenue. Make it tangible.

## 2. AI-Driven Coding Is Changing What Small Teams Can Build

*The pitch mentioned this — expand on how AI coding tools democratize software development.*

**Key talking points:**

- **The new software development lifecycle.** AI coding assistants (Copilot, Cursor, Claude) are letting small teams punch above their weight. A 3-person startup can now build what used to require 10 engineers. This is the biggest unlock for SMBs since cloud computing.

- **But it's not magic.** You still need someone who understands architecture, security, and what "good" looks like. AI coding tools amplify skill — they don't replace it. The danger is shipping code nobody can maintain.

- **Practical advice for SMB owners:** Start with AI-assisted prototyping. Use it to validate ideas faster. The cost of experimentation just dropped by 10x.

**Story to tell:** A concrete example from your own experience of how AI coding tools changed a team's velocity or capability.

## 3. The Job Loss Panic Serves VCs, Not Reality

*Provocative angle — perfect for this audience of business owners worried about the future.*

**Key talking points:**

- **Who benefits from the "AI will replace everyone" narrative?** VCs. It creates urgency to invest, urgency to buy, urgency to panic-adopt. That urgency drives inflated valuations and rushed implementations that fail.

- **What the data actually shows.** Jobs are changing, not disappearing. The Highmark example: underwriters didn't lose their jobs — they became reviewers and strategists. The work got better, not eliminated.

- **The solopreneur advantage.** If you're a one-person business, AI isn't replacing you — it's giving you leverage you never had. Content creation, customer research, financial modeling, scheduling — all augmented, not automated.

- **Regulated industry perspective.** In healthcare and defense, AI adoption is slower but stickier. The jobs didn't disappear — they evolved. That pattern will repeat across every industry.

**Story to tell:** The Highmark underwriter transformation — 30-year veterans learning to work with ML systems, becoming more effective, not obsolete.

---

## 4. Start Small, Spend Wisely, Scale Smart

*Mirror the show's tagline back to them with practical frameworks.*

**Key talking points:**

- **The $0 AI audit.** Before spending anything, catalog your repetitive tasks. Which ones are high-volume, low-complexity, and tolerate some error? Those are your AI candidates.

- **Commodity vs. custom.** 90% of SMBs should use off-the-shelf AI tools. ChatGPT, Claude, Zapier AI, etc. Custom ML is for when AI is your product, not your tool.

- **The pilot mentality.** Pick one process, one tool, one month. Measure the result in hours saved or revenue gained. Then decide whether to expand.

**Story to tell:** Contrast between enterprise AI deployments (expensive, slow, complex) and what an SMB can do with $50/month and good judgment.
