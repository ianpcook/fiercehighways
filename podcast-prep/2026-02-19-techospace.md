## 1. The New Software Development Lifecycle in the Age of AI

*This was the pitch angle that hooked Akhil — go deep on this for the tech-savvy audience.*

**Key talking points:**

- **How AI is reshaping engineering teams.** Coding assistants aren't just autocomplete — they're changing how teams architect, review, and ship. The development cycle is compressing. What used to take sprints takes days.

- **The Qloo engineering perspective.** Building AI products that serve Fortune 500 clients requires a different development discipline. Provider-agnostic LLM infrastructure, scalable data pipelines, real-time preference prediction — the engineering challenges are in reliability, not novelty.

- **The skills shift for tech leaders.** Engineering managers need to evaluate AI-assisted output, not just human output. Code review changes when half the code comes from Copilot. Quality assurance evolves. The CTO's job is now partly about curating AI-human collaboration patterns.

- **What "AI-native" development actually looks like.** Not just using AI tools — building workflows where AI is embedded in every stage: design, implementation, testing, deployment, monitoring.

**Story to tell:** A specific engineering workflow at Qloo or a previous role where AI tools materially changed how the team operated.

## 2. Life in the Long Tail of Non-Unicorn Startups

*The other pitch angle — this resonates with tech leaders who've lived through startup reality.*

**Key talking points:**

- **The startup survival narrative nobody tells.** Most AI startups aren't raising $100M rounds. They're grinding through product-market fit, pivoting when the market shifts, and trying to build something sustainable. That's where the real innovation happens.

- **The Sixfold story.** A real pivot under pressure — what triggered it, how the technical team adapted, what was salvaged and what was rebuilt. The honest version, not the LinkedIn version.

- **Building AI products in constrained environments.** Small teams, limited budgets, enterprise clients with demanding requirements. This is where engineering discipline matters most.

- **The CTO's dilemma: innovation vs. sustainability.** When do you bet on new tech vs. doubling down on what works? How do you manage technical debt while shipping features?

**Story to tell:** The Sixfold pivot — the market signal that forced the change, the technical decisions that followed, and what the team learned about building under uncertainty.

## 3. Bridging Technical Teams and Executive Vision

*Tech leaders in this audience live this tension daily.*

**Key talking points:**

- **Translating AI capabilities for the boardroom.** The RAND experience — presenting complex analytics to defense policymakers who need actionable intelligence, not model architectures. That translation skill is the most valuable in any tech leader's toolkit.

- **The Highmark example.** Getting 30-year insurance underwriting veterans to trust an ML system. The technical challenge was 20% of the problem. The other 80% was change management, trust-building, and process redesign.

- **Managing up and down simultaneously.** Executives want ROI timelines. Engineers want architectural integrity. The tech leader's job is to protect both while shipping.

**Story to tell:** A specific board presentation or executive stakeholder conversation where translating technical complexity into business language changed the outcome.
