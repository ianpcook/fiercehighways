## 1. AI in Advertising and Personalization — The Qloo Angle

*This is their bread and butter. Connect your work directly to their world.*

**Key talking points:**

- **What Qloo does for the advertising ecosystem.** Consumer preference prediction across entertainment, dining, travel, fashion. This is the data layer that powers smarter ad targeting, content recommendations, and audience understanding — without creepy surveillance.

- **Taste graphs vs. behavior tracking.** The ad industry is moving away from cookies and tracking pixels. Preference prediction offers a privacy-friendly alternative: understanding what people *like* rather than what they *click*. This is the future of targeting.

- **The data quality crisis in ad tech.** Most ad targeting is built on garbage data. Qloo's approach — structured preference data across domains — shows what's possible when you invest in data infrastructure instead of just buying more impressions.

- **Personalization that works vs. personalization theater.** Most "personalized" experiences are shallow — "you bought shoes, here are more shoes." Real personalization connects preferences across domains: dining taste predicts entertainment choices, travel patterns reveal fashion preferences.

**Story to tell:** A Qloo use case where cross-domain preference data produced targeting or personalization results that traditional ad tech couldn't match.

## 2. The Substance vs. Vaporware Filter

*Brett asked for "provocative." Deliver.*

**Key talking points:**

- **How to spot AI vaporware.** Three red flags: (1) They demo but won't let you test with your data. (2) They talk about "AGI" or "superintelligence" in their pitch deck. (3) Their pricing model depends on you being locked in before you can measure results.

- **The enterprise AI sales playbook exposed.** Most AI vendors sell the dream, deliver a POC, then charge for "customization" indefinitely. The ad industry is particularly susceptible because the ROI metrics are already fuzzy.

- **Why the AGI conversation is a sideshow for businesses.** Every minute a CMO spends worrying about AGI is a minute not spent on the AI that could optimize their media mix today. The real opportunity is in boring, narrow AI applied consistently.

- **The "AI strategy" tax.** Companies paying consultancies $500K for AI strategy decks when they should be spending $50K on a pilot that actually proves value.

**Story to tell:** An example (anonymized) of an enterprise that fell for vaporware vs. one that took the boring, disciplined approach and got real results.

## 3. The Data and Privacy Landscape — From Defense to Consumer

*Unique angle: Ian's RAND background gives him credibility on data governance that most AI guests can't match.*

**Key talking points:**

- **What the ad industry can learn from defense data practices.** At RAND, data provenance, auditability, and access controls weren't optional — they were mission-critical. The ad industry is heading toward similar requirements (GDPR, state privacy laws, cookie deprecation) but without the discipline.

- **Compliance as competitive advantage.** Companies that build privacy-first data infrastructure now will own the market when the regulatory hammer falls. This echoes the healthcare lesson from Highmark.

- **The streaming/media data opportunity.** The explosion of streaming data creates new preference signals. Companies that can ethically aggregate and interpret this data will define the next era of media intelligence.

**Story to tell:** How the discipline of working with classified data at RAND shaped a different approach to data governance that translates directly to today's privacy-first advertising world.
