## 2. AI Adoption That Actually Works — Revenue, Costs, Risk

*Match the show's framework: grow revenue, cut costs, manage risk.*

**Key talking points:**

- **Grow revenue: Qloo's approach.** Consumer preference prediction helps Fortune 500 clients make better decisions about what to offer and where. The revenue impact comes from understanding taste at scale — connecting entertainment, dining, travel, and fashion preferences to unlock cross-selling and personalization.

- **Cut costs: The Highmark story.** 97% reduction in underwriting time. The cost savings weren't just labor — they were in error reduction, faster processing, and improved customer satisfaction. The AI paid for itself in months, not years.

- **Manage risk: Defense and compliance lessons.** At RAND, AI outputs had to be auditable, explainable, and defensible. At Highmark, regulatory compliance wasn't a barrier — it was the architecture that made the system trustworthy. Risk management isn't a cost center — it's the foundation of sustainable AI.

**Story to tell:** Pick one from each category and make it vivid. The audience wants to see themselves in the story.

## 3. The Hype vs. Reality Gap — What Leaders Get Wrong

*The show's audience is evaluating AI for their businesses. Help them avoid mistakes.*

**Key talking points:**

- **The three most common AI adoption mistakes:** (1) Starting with technology instead of problems. (2) Hiring a data science team before having clean data. (3) Measuring AI success in model metrics instead of business KPIs.

- **The vendor evaluation filter.** Ask: can I see this working with my data in 30 days? If the answer is "we need 6 months to customize," walk away. The best AI solutions prove value fast.

- **Why regulated industry experience matters.** Companies that have built AI under regulatory scrutiny (healthcare, defense, finance) have battle-tested playbooks. The unregulated market is now catching up to the same requirements — privacy, explainability, auditability.

**Story to tell:** An anonymized example of a company that got it right vs. one that got it wrong, and what differentiated them.
